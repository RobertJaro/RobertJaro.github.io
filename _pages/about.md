Robert Jarolim is a NASA Jack Eddy Postdoctoral Fellow at the High Altitude Observatory (NCAR) in Boulder, Colorado. His current work focuses on physics-informed machine learning for heliophysics, especially simulation and reconstruction of solar corona and heliosphere.

He completed his PhD in Physics at the University of Graz (2021-2023), with the dissertation *Frontiers of Artificial Intelligence in Solar Physics* (passed with distinction). Before that, he earned an MSc in Physics (2018-2020) and a BSc in Physics (2015-2018), both with distinction, and trained in Computer Science at HTL Kaindorf/Sulm.

His research combines solar physics with modern AI methods across:
- magnetic field simulations
- tomographic reconstructions of the solar corona
- image enhancement and instrument-to-instrument translation
- automated solar feature detection
- spectropolarimetry and Milne Eddington inversions with physics-informed neural networks

Robert has contributed to projects and programs including SOLARNET (EU Horizon 2020), NASA MDRAIT (Instrument-to-Instrument tool), and Frontier Development Lab collaborations with ESA/ESRIN, NASA, and the SETI Institute.
