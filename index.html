Next-Generation Solar Physics with AI

I am a NASA Jack Eddy Postdoctoral Fellow at NCAR's High Altitude Observatory in Boulder. My research combines heliophysics, machine learning, and scientific computing to model solar magnetic fields, reconstruct the Sun in 3D, enhance solar imaging data, and automate solar feature detection for space weather applications.

Project Overview

Magnetic Field Simulations

Physics-informed neural networks and force-free field modeling to improve reconstruction of coronal magnetic structures.

Tomographic Reconstructions

3D reconstruction of the solar EUV corona with neural radiance field approaches such as SuNeRF.

Image Enhancement

Deep learning methods for cross-instrument translation and quality enhancement in ground-based solar observations.

Solar Feature Detection

Automated detection of coronal holes and related solar structures from multi-channel image streams.

Spectropolarimetric Inversions

Inversion workflows for retrieving magnetic and atmospheric parameters from polarimetric solar measurements.
